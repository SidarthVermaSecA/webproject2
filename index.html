Financial institutions offer several types of cards to meet various needs. Credit cards allow users to borrow money up to a certain limit and often come with rewards programs. Debit cards are linked to a bank account, letting users spend their own funds. Prepaid cards require users to load money onto them beforehand and can be used similarly to debit cards. Charge cards also offer borrowing but require the balance to be paid in full each month. ATM cards are primarily for cash withdrawals, while secured credit cards require a deposit as collateral to secure the credit limit.

Interest is the cost of borrowing money, typically expressed as a percentage of the loan amount. It compensates the lender for the risk and opportunity cost associated with lending funds. Banks charge interest to cover their operating expenses, manage risk, and earn a profit. For borrowers, interest represents the cost of accessing funds now rather than saving and paying for them later. For banks, interest is a primary source of revenue, helping them fund their own operations and provide services.

To change your credit card's 4-digit PIN, you can typically do so through your online banking account or mobile app by finding the PIN change option. Alternatively, you can call the customer service number on your card for assistance or visit an ATM if your card supports this feature. If needed, you can also go to your bank's branch and ask a representative to help with the PIN change. Make sure to select a secure PIN and avoid easily guessable numbers.
